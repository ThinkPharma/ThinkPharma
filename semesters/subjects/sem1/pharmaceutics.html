5 Units

📘 PCI Syllabus

Unit 1

Introduction to Dosage Forms

10 Hours
  • History of pharmacy profession
  • Introduction to dosage forms and their classification
  • Pharmacopoeias - IP, BP, USP
  • Drug delivery systems - conventional and novel
  • Prescription - types and handling
Unit 2

Posology & Pharmaceutical Calculations

8 Hours
  • Posology - factors affecting dose
  • Pediatric and geriatric dosing
  • Pharmaceutical calculations - percentage, ratio
  • Allegation method
  • Isotonicity calculations
Unit 3

Powders

9 Hours
  • Advantages and disadvantages of powders
  • Classification of powders
  • Simple and compound powders
  • Effervescent powders and granules
  • Dusting powders and insufflations
Unit 4

Monophasic Liquid Dosage Forms

10 Hours
  • Solvents - aqueous and non-aqueous
  • Solutions, syrups and elixirs
  • Aromatic waters and spirits
  • Linctuses and drops
  • Gargles and mouthwashes
Unit 5

Biphasic Liquid Dosage Forms

8 Hours
  • Suspensions - types and formulation
  • Flocculated and deflocculated systems
  • Emulsions - types and theories
  • Stability of emulsions
  • HLB system and emulsifying agents
