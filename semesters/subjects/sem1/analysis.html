Unit 1

Introduction & Errors

8 Hours
  • Introduction to Pharmaceutical Analysis

  • Methods of expressing concentration

  • Accuracy, Precision and Significant figures
Unit 2

Acid-Base Titrations

10 Hours
  • Theories of acids and bases
  • Buffers and buffer capacity
  • Neutralization curves
  • Acid-base indicators and their selection
  • Applications in Pharmacy
Unit 3

Oxidation-Reduction Titrations

10 Hours
  • Concepts of oxidation and reduction
  • Redox indicators
  • Permanganometry and Cerimetry
  • Iodimetry and Iodometry
  • Applications in pharmaceutical analysis

Precipitation Titrations

8 Hours
  • Precipitation titration theory
  • Mohr's method
  • Volhard's method
  • Fajan's method
  • Applications in pharmaceutical analysis

Complexometric Titrations

9 Hours
  • Complexation and chelation
  • Metal ion indicators
  • EDTA as chelating agent
  • Types of complexometric titrations
  • Pharmaceutical applications
